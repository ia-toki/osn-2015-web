Pertahanan Yogya

Time limit: 1000 ms

Memory limit: 65536 KB

 

Description

Yogya is in danger! An evil wants to cover Yogya by the forces of darkness. Pak Dengklek cannot stay still. He seeks to defeat and expel the evil even though this is not an easy task. The evil is a very strong fighter and it has a lot of soldiers.

As a senior fighter, Pak Dengklek knew that he should act carefully. Fortunately he has a good ability to analyze and he has good skills how to fight against evil creatures. For convenience, all creatures are numbered from 1 through N. All creatures' fighting skill is expressed in a pair of numbers Si and Ti, where Si is the stamina and Ti is the power. The evil is represented by number 1, whereas its soldiers are represented by number i, i > 1. Pak Dengklek also has his own fighting skills, expressed by Sd and Td.

The battles are described as follows: Pak Dengklek attacks an enemny, reducing the enemy's stamina with Td. Then the enemy attacks Pak Dengklek, reducing Pak Dengklek's stamina with Ti. The process is repeated continuously until one of them runs out of stamina (stamina ≤ 0), where he is declared the loser.

Pak Dengklek realizes that the evil is very strong, so he plans to beat its soldiers before the evil. Every time Pak Dengklek beats an enemy, its ability will grow by Ls and Lt. It means that his stamina is incremented by Ls, and his power is incremented by Lt. Every time Pak Dengklek completes a battle, he takes a rest so that his stamina is recovered.

Although Pak Dengklek becomes stronger when he beats more soldiers, he also wants to win the battle in a short time. You are asked to help Pak Dengklek to arrange the order of soldiers to be killed for winning over the evil, in such a way that the number of soldiers that need to be beaten is minimum.

Input Format

The first line contains the test case label. A test case label is a string with the following description:

  • The length of the string is the number of subtasks plus one. The 0-th character (index is started from 0) is '0' if the test case is a sample test case, otherwise it is '.' (dot).
  • For each i from 1 to the number of subtasks:
    • If the test case satisfies the constraints of the i-th subtask, then the i-th character is i, or
    • If the test case does not satisfy the constraints of the i-th subtask, then the i-th character is '.' (dot).

For example, if the test case label of a task is 0..345, then:

  • The task has 5 subtasks,
  • The test case is a sample test case, and
  • The test case satisfies the constraints of the 3rd, 4th, and 5th subtasks.

The next line contains 5 integers: Sd, Td, Ls, Lt, dan N.

Each of the next N lines contains two integers Si dan Ti representing the stamina and the power of the i-th enemy. The first enemy is the evil.

Output Format

Output an integer Q, the minimum number of soldiers that must be beaten by Pak Dengklek before beating the evil. The next Q lines are index i (1 < i ≤ N), the order of soldiers that must be beaten by pak Dengklek. If there are more than one possible orders, output any of them.

If it is impossible that Pak Dengklek can beat the evil, output a single line consisting of -1.

2

Explanation of Sample Input

Firstly, Pak Dengklek kills the enemy number 3. After that, the stamina of Pak Dengklek is 12 and his power is 5. Then, Pak Dengklek kills the enemy number 2, so that his stamina and power are increasing to 14 and 5. With this stamina and power, Pak Dengklek will be able to beat the evil (enemy number 1).

  • 1 ≤ Sd, Td, Si, Ti ≤ 100.000
  • 0 ≤ Ls, Lt ≤ 100.000

Description

Pak Dengklek has a very wide rice field. Interestingly, the rice field is located in a mountain hill area, so that the tenements on the field have a variability in altitudes. Pak Dengklek’s rice field has N x M tenements in size. The top-left tenement of the rice field is labeled [1,1] and the bottom-right tenement is labeled [N,M]. Each tenement in Pak Dengklek’s rice field has the altitude of T[i,j].

To make Pak Dengklek's rice field fertile, he has to do watering every day. Due to the variability in altitude among the tenements, whenever Pak Dengklek is watering a tenement, the water will flow to the tenements with lower altitudes. Specifically, when Pak Dengklek is watering the tenement [i,j], the water will flow to a neighboring tenement of [i,j] if the altitude of the neighboring tenement is lower than the altitude of [i,j]. The neighbors of a tenement [i,j] are the four tenements that share the same border with tenement [i,j]. The water will flow further, until it is in a tenement where all of its neighbors have higher altitudes.

Bisa Jadi Tebak Angka

Time limit: 100 ms

Memory limit: 32768 KB

 

Description

Pak Dengklek's ducks are having a tour in Yogyakarta. Now, they are in the Ngayogyakarta Hadiningrat Palace. Besides being the residence of the Sultan, the Palace also functions as a museum which displays various collection of objects belong to the Sultan, ranging from gifts given by several European kingdoms, sacred replicas of the Palace, as well as gamelan (a traditional musical instrument) which is played in a very special occasion. Mr. Dengklek’s ducks are certainly interested in all that objects.

All ducks, except Kwak and Kwek. While the other ducks are enjoying themselves exploring the Palace, Kwak and Kwek feel bored and just stay under a tree shade. Finally they decided to play guessing a number while they are waiting for the others.

The game is played as follows. Initially, Kwak is thinking of an integer between 1 and N, inclusive (suppose that the number is X). Kwek has to make a guess on the value of X. Kwek does this by asking a sequence of questions.

In each question, Kwek gives a set of integers S to Kwak. Then, Kwak has to give one out of the three possible answers as follows:

  • YA (means "YES"), if X is in the set S.

  • BISAJADI (means "MAYBE"), if X is not in the set S, but X+1 or X-1 is in the set S.

  • TIDAK (means "NO"), if the two conditions above are not satisfied.

In the end, Kwek has to guess the integer that Kwak is thinking of. If the guess is equal to X, then Kwek wins the game.

Of course, Kwek and Kwak can not play this game for too long, because the other ducks aree coming shortly and will continue the tour to other point of interest in Yogyakarta. Therefore, they put a constraint to this game: Kwek can only ask a maximum of K questions before guessing the integer that Kwak is thinking of.

Please help Kwek win this game!

Interaction Format

The first line contains the test case label. A test case label is a string with the following description:

  • The length of the string is the number of subtasks plus one. The 0-th character (index is started from 0) is '0' if the test case is a sample test case, otherwise it is '.' (dot).
  • For each i from 1 to the number of subtasks:
    • If the test case satisfies the constraints of the i-th subtask, then the i-th character is i, or
    • If the test case does not satisfy the constraints of the i-th subtask, then the i-th character is '.' (dot).

For example, if the test case label of a task is 0..345, then:

  • The task has 5 subtasks,
  • The test case is a sample test case, and
  • The test case satisfies the constraints of the 3rd, 4th, and 5th subtasks.

Your program should read two integers N and K. Afterwards, your program should do a sequence of actions, each of which is one of the following:

  • Guessing. Your program should output a line consisting of 0 Y. That is, the integer 0 followed by your guess.

  • Asking. Your program should output a line consisting of M S1 S2 ... SM that is, an integer M followed by a set of M integers that you are asking.

Every time your program is taking a guess, your program will be ensured to read one of the three possible strings YA, BISAJADI, dan TIDAK, which corresponds to the definition explained in the problem description.

Make sure that your program quits the interaction after guessing an integer that Kwak is thinking of.

Ayam Aneh

Time limit: 100 ms

Memory limit: 32768 KB

 

Description

To make their trip efficient, Pak Dengklek and his ducks stay the night in Pak Blangkon’s house. Pak Dengklek see Pak Blangkon's farm which is full of chickens. Some of them are red, some are white. Everyday, Pak Blangkon feeds his chickens with special food that will mutate the DNA of the chickens so that the color of the chickens may change.

Seeing the colorful chickens, Pak Dengklek is interested to change the color of his ducks. Pak Dengklek consults Pak Blangkon about how to change the DNA of the chickens. Instead of giving the answer directly, Pak Blangkon asks Pak Dengklek to guess the DNA of the chickens.  

DNA is represented as a string of characters, all of which are in capital character from A to Z. All same characters in the string are located next to each other. For example, AAASSSDDDFF is a DNA string. while AAASSSDDDAAFFF is not a DNA because between two groups of A's there exist S's and D's.

This is the way how Pak Dengklek should guess the DNA: Pak Blangkon gives a integer N; i.e., length of the DNA string of a chicken which Pak Dengklek wish to know its DNA. In addition, Pak Blangkon also gives an integer K, representing the maximum number of questions that can be asked by Pak Dengklek before he guesses the DNA string of a the chicken.

Every time Pak Dengklek asks a question, he gives it as a string. As a response, Pak Blangkon answers with YA (means YES) or TIDAK (means NO) depending on whether the string that Pak Dengklek asked is indeed the substring of the DNA of the chicken in question. Note: X is a substring of Y if X consists of at least one character, and X can be obtained by removing zero or more characters forming prefix of Y and zero of more characters forming suffix of Y. For example, BEBE, EBE, BEK, and BEBEK are substrings of BEBEK, whereas BEEK is not a subtring of BEBEK.

Pak Dengklek is puzzled by the way pak Blangkon set the interaction protocol. Please help Pak Dengklek guess the DNA of the chicken
